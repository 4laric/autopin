# Egypt + Hatshepsut — Antiquity Playbook

**Difficulty:** Immortal · **Focus:** Cultural (Wonders of the Ancient World — build 7 wonders) · **Bank on the side:** Economic

The reason you vibe and lose is that Antiquity has three scoring clocks running at once (culture, science, economic, plus military/expansion legacy) and no one tells you when each thing needs to happen. This playbook gives you a *default* line and the handful of decision points where you should deviate. Follow the defaults; deviate only when the trigger fires.

---

## The one-sentence identity

You are a **wide-ish, river-hugging, wonder factory**. Hatshepsut gives **+15% Production toward Buildings and Wonders in cities next to Navigable Rivers**, plus **+1 Culture per Age per unique Resource**. Egypt gives **+1 Production on Navigable Rivers** and **+30% Production toward the Pyramids**. Everything you do keys off *navigable rivers* and *wonders*. If a decision doesn't feed wonders, gold, or culture, it's probably wrong for this game.

**Win condition:** 7 wonders completed before the Age ends = Wonders of the Ancient World (cultural) legacy path complete → Golden Age. Everything below is in service of hitting 7 comfortably (aim to have 5+ locked in by the two-thirds mark so a wonder-race loss doesn't sink you).

---

## Pre-game / turn 1 setup

**Map/settings that make your life easier (if you're choosing):** any map with continents/rivers. You want navigable rivers. Standard speed. If you can see your start, look for **desert tiles next to a navigable river** — that's Egypt's dream (desert triggers Mastaba's gold adjacency, river triggers all your production bonuses).

**Turn 1 decisions:**

1. **Where to settle the capital.** Do NOT reflex-settle in place. Move at most 1 tile to get **adjacent to a navigable river**. A river-adjacent capital is worth losing a turn for. Secondary wants, in order: fresh water, a couple of resources in range, some flat/desert land for districts, not boxed in by mountains. Don't chase a "perfect" spot past turn 2 — the production tempo matters more.
2. **First build: Scout.** Then usually a **second Scout**. On Immortal you're behind on map knowledge and need to find (a) good second-city land near rivers, (b) resources to trade for, (c) where the AI is so you can pre-position defense. Scouts reveal hidden tiles — send them in different directions.
3. **First tech:** see tech section — default **Pottery**.
4. **First civic:** **Mysticism** (details below).

## City founding rules (this is where most games are quietly lost)

- **Every city next to a navigable river if humanly possible.** No river = no Hatshepsut/Egypt production bonus = you are playing a bad generic civ. A slightly worse river spot beats a great non-river spot.
- Egypt has a **lower settlement cap** and isn't expansionist — so **quality over quantity**. Aim for ~3–4 well-placed cities you actually develop rather than sprawling. Towns (the auto-developing settlement type) are fine for filling economic/food gaps; **cities** (specialized) are where wonders and unique quarters go.
- Put your **wonder-factory city** (capital, ideally) on the best river+desert combo. That city builds most of your 7 wonders.
- Grab tiles with **unique resources** — each one is +1 Culture/Age from Hatshepsut and a trade chip for the economic path.

**Decision — settle vs. town:** if the spot has a river + room for districts/wonders → found a **city**. If it's just good food/resources filling a gap → **town**. Don't make every settlement a city; cities cost more to keep productive.

## Tech order (Antiquity)

Priorities, in order. Adjust to what your terrain actually gives you.

1. **Pottery** — Granary + economy foundation. (You unlock the Granary very early; build it after your first scout or two.)
2. **Writing** — Library → **Codices** (this is the *Science* legacy currency; you want some of it even on a culture game because the tech/civic payoffs compound). Get **Writing 2** soon after for a free codex + better libraries.
3. **Masonry** — walls (defense on Immortal) + production buildings, and it's on the path to wonders.
4. **Currency** — markets/gold, feeds the economic side-quest and lets you buy your way out of trouble.
5. From here, **beeline whatever techs unlock the wonders you're targeting** and the Medjay (your unique defensive infantry). Techs and civics each unlock different wonders — don't tunnel one tree.

**Rule of thumb:** research whatever most increases **production** first when in doubt (production builds wonders), then whatever unlocks your **next wonder** or your **unique buildings**.

## Civic order (Antiquity) — including Egypt's unique tree

**Generic civics first:**

1. **Mysticism** — top priority. Unlocks altars and, crucially, the ability to found a **Pantheon**. Grab a strong pantheon (e.g. a production or culture one; **Stone Circles**-type effects are excellent). The pantheon carries value into Exploration.
2. **Mysticism 2** — +2 food everywhere helps towns reach 7 pop (unlocks their specialization). Strong and cheap.
3. **Discipline** (or a strong unique civic) — the **free Commander** is worth it on Immortal for defense; a commander massively multiplies your handful of Medjay.

**Egypt's unique civic tree — this is your wonder engine, prioritize it early:**

The tree is **Arrival of Hapi → Scales of Anubis → Light of Amun-Ra**. The first two civics unlock your unique buildings:

- **Mortuary Temple** — +4 Gold base, **Happiness adjacency for navigable rivers and wonders**.
- **Mastaba** — +3 Culture base, **Gold adjacency for desert and wonders**.

Build the Mastaba and Mortuary Temple **on the same tile** to form the **Necropolis** unique quarter. The Necropolis:

- Gives **Gold + Happiness bonuses next to wonder tiles**,
- Pays out **~+100 Gold every time you complete a wonder** (scales with game speed),
- Unlocks **Tjaty** (Egypt's Great People, only producible after the Necropolis).

**Why this matters for your plan:** the Necropolis turns your wonder-spam into a self-funding gold machine. Every wonder you build for the culture path *also* pays gold (economic path) and happiness (keeps you stable while wide). Get the Necropolis built **before** you go all-in on wonders so each wonder double-dips.

**Civic sequencing in practice:** Mysticism → (start Egyptian tree: Arrival of Hapi) → Mysticism 2 → Scales of Anubis (finish the Necropolis unlock) → Discipline/commander → Light of Amun-Ra → rest of the tree / economic civics.

## The wonder plan (the actual win)

**Target: 7 wonders.** You have a huge production edge, so you can win the race even against the AI *if you don't dawdle*.

**Sequencing logic:**

1. **Pyramids first** whenever you can — you get **+30% production** on it (Egypt) *stacked with* Hatshepsut's +15% river bonus. This is the single most cost-efficient wonder in the game for you. Almost nobody out-races Egypt to the Pyramids.
2. **Build wonders in your river city** so the +15% always applies. Cluster them: each wonder tile boosts your Mastaba (gold) and Mortuary Temple (happiness) adjacencies, and the Necropolis pays out per wonder.
3. **Watch the wonder race.** Wonders are finite — one of each, first to finish wins, and you lose the invested production if someone beats you. On Immortal the AI *will* race you. Two defenses:
   - Keep a **production surplus** so you can pivot a wonder mid-build if the AI is about to finish it.
   - **Chop/rush** (use production-boosting tiles, purchase surrounding buildings) into a wonder you *must* have.
4. **Don't build all 7 in one city** if it slows you — a second river city building wonders in parallel finishes the set faster. But your Necropolis city should host the wonder cluster for adjacency value.
5. **Antiquity wonders that exist to target** include the Great Pyramid of Giza, Colossus of Rhodes, Lighthouse of Alexandria, Mausoleum at Halicarnassus, Hanging Gardens of Babylon, Temple of Artemis, Statue of Zeus. You only need 7 total across the age — take the ones you can win the race for and that buff gold/culture/production.

**Milestone check (keep yourself honest):**

- Early third of the age: **Pyramids underway or done**, Necropolis unlocked, 3–4 cities founded on rivers.
- Middle third: **2–3 wonders done**, Necropolis built and paying out, pantheon founded.
- Final third: **grinding out wonders 4–7**, defending your cities, banking economic points from all that wonder gold.

## Economic side-quest (free points, do it passively)

You'll pick up the economic legacy almost for free:

- **Import resources via trade deals.** More imported resources = more culture (accelerates civics) *and* progress on the economic path. Actively propose trades.
- **Unique resources** you own each give **+1 Culture/Age** (Hatshepsut). Prioritize settling/claiming them.
- The **Necropolis gold** (+100/wonder) and Mortuary Temple/Market gold snowball your treasury — use it to **buy buildings/units** to accelerate wonders and defense.
- Don't *chase* economic at the cost of wonders; it's the thing you bank while doing the main plan.

## Defense on Immortal (don't get run over)

Egypt's military is weak — this is your real risk on Immortal.

- **Build early Masonry walls** in border/coastal cities. A walled city with a garrison usually holds vs. AI Antiquity stacks.
- **Medjay** (Egypt's unique infantry) get **+combat strength on home territory** and can later camp on urban districts for gold — they're your defensive backbone. Don't send them off adventuring; keep them home.
- Get the **free commander** from Discipline and keep 2–3 defensive units near your wonder city. Losing your wonder-factory city loses the game.
- **Don't over-invest in offense.** You're not conquering; you're surviving so your wonders finish. Peace and walls > armies.
- If an AI is clearly hostile and stronger, **buy units with your wonder-gold** and turtle. Your economy lets you emergency-defend in a way most civs can't.

## Fast decision cheat-sheet

Use this when you're mid-turn and don't want to think hard.

- **New city?** → Is it on a navigable river? If no, is there a river spot within reach? Prefer river even if slightly worse.
- **What to build next in my wonder city?** → Wonder available I can win the race on? Build it. Necropolis not built yet? Build Mastaba/Mortuary Temple. Else: production/gold building, or bank a wonder for next turn.
- **What to research (tech)?** → Does it unlock a wonder I want or the Medjay? Else the thing that most raises production.
- **What to research (civic)?** → Egyptian unique tree until Necropolis is unlocked; Mysticism line for pantheon; then economic/commander.
- **AI about to finish "my" wonder?** → Pivot to a different one, or dump gold to rush-buy support and win the race. Never eat a full loss on a contested wonder.
- **Getting attacked?** → Walls + Medjay + a bought unit or two. Turtle. Don't counter-invade.
- **Idle gold piling up?** → Buy buildings/units to speed wonders or shore up defense. Idle gold is wasted tempo.

## What "losing by vibing" usually looks like — and the fix

- **Cities not on rivers** → you threw away both your production bonuses. *Fix: river discipline, always.*
- **Too many cities, none developed** → Egypt punishes sprawl. *Fix: 3–4 real cities, towns for the rest.*
- **Started wonders too late / lost races** → *Fix: Pyramids ASAP, Necropolis early, keep a production buffer to pivot.*
- **No pantheon / ignored civics** → *Fix: Mysticism first, always.*
- **Got wiped because "Egypt has no army"** → *Fix: walls + Medjay + a commander is enough to hold; you don't need more.*
- **Hoarded gold doing nothing** → *Fix: spend it to buy tempo.*
