# THE TENURE TRACK

### Confucius · Han → Ming → Meiji Japan · Scientific Victory (Innovation Race)

**Difficulty: Immortal/Deity · Posture: strictly non-aggressive · Verified against 1.4.0 "Test of Time" (May 19, 2026) + Update 1.4.1 (June 23, 2026)**

You are not conquering the world. You are out-publishing it. Every city is a campus, every citizen who matters is faculty, and the endgame is a rocket with your name on the grant. The Slumlord packed bodies into towns for rent; the Tenure Track packs specialists into quarters for citations. Same hoarding instinct, better funding.

One warning up front, because you're stepping up from Sovereign: on Deity the AI gets **+80% Science, Culture, Gold, and Happiness, +80% building/wonder production, +160% unit production, a flat +8 Combat Strength, +100% unit XP, and pays 40% less for techs and civics** (Immortal: +60% yields, +120% unit production, +5 CS, −30% costs). You will be behind on raw beakers until mid-Exploration. That's fine. The AI cheats on yields; it does not cheat on specialist math, and it is famously bad at the Innovation race. Your job is to survive its early tantrums and let compound interest do the rest.

---

## 1. Leader: Confucius

**Ability — Keju:** +25% Growth Rate in all Cities, and **+2 Science from every Specialist.**

That's the whole engine in one line. Post-1.4.0, specialists have **no base yield** — they now give 100% of their tile's adjacency yields instead (up from 50%). That rework gutted lazy specialist spam for everyone else, but Keju's flat +2 Science per head is untouched, which quietly made Confucius one of the best pure-science leaders in the game. Every specialist you place is +2 Science before the tile even does anything, and the +25% growth means you generate the growth events (which is how specialists get placed) faster than anyone.

**Agenda — Guanxi:** AI Confucius likes whoever has the most specialists. Irrelevant to you except as flavor — you *will* have the most specialists.

**Attributes:** Scientific + Expansionist. Pump the Scientific tree first — it's where specialist-limit and science-multiplier nodes live. The level-3 leader-path reward (+50% Gold toward converting Towns to Cities) is on-theme: cheaper campus openings.

**Why him over a "stronger" meta leader:** the build never asks you to fight, expand aggressively, or chase treasure fleets. It asks you to grow tall, place specialists, and not die. Keju does the first two by itself.

## 2. The Civ Path

### Antiquity — Han
**Unlock:** Confucius's suggested civ; available from the start.
**Ability — Nine Provinces:** your Capital gains a free Population every time it completes a Science Building; the Capital and new Towns get an extra Population on their first growth event. Free pop = free future specialists.

- **Shi Dafu** (unique great person, trainable in cities of 10+ pop): hands out Codices, bonus Science, free techs, Celebrations. **Slotted Codices generate Innovation** — actual Scientific Victory points — starting in Antiquity.
- **Chu-Ko-Nu** (unique archer): exerts zone of control, +5 ranged strength vs adjacent units. A *defensive* unique unit. On Deity, that ZOC is how four units hold a river line against twelve.
- **Great Wall** (unique improvement): Culture, Ageless Happiness, counts as a Fortification (+6 CS to defenders), 25% cost-scaling per copy. A literal wall between you and the nearest warmonger that also pays rent.
- Han's civic tree carries specialist-science traditions (Ju Xian line) you can slot forever.

### Exploration — Ming
**Unlock:** playing Han unlocks it, *and* Confucius unlocks it independently — double-covered. (Generic route: improve 3 Silk or slot 8 resources in one settlement.)
**Ability — Great Canon of Yongle:** **+50% Science in the Capital**, +1 Tradition slot, +30% Production toward the Forbidden City, but **−15 Science per turn for each Social Policy (not Tradition) slotted.**

Read the trade carefully: Ming pays you to run *Traditions* instead of generic policies. The extra Tradition slot plus Han's carried-over specialist traditions means you barely feel the penalty, while your capital — where all the Nine Provinces free pop went — gets its science half-again multiplied. Capital-super-university is the play.

- **Ming Great Wall:** +5 Culture, +1 Gold per adjacent Fortification, counts as a Fortification. Chain it along your exposed border.
- **Xunleichong** (ranged, +4 CS on featureless tiles) covers the flat approaches your walls don't.

### Modern — Meiji Japan
**Unlock — THIS IS THE ONE YOU CAN FUMBLE:** Ming does *not* unlock Meiji (Ming's natural heir is Qing). Confucius doesn't either. You unlock Meiji by **improving 3 Tea resources before the Modern Age.** Tea is an Exploration Age resource (+1 Science and Production in Cities, doubled in Homelands), so the moment you enter Exploration, scout for Tea and prioritize settling/improving three. Treat it like a quest objective. (Brush & Blade DLC: playing Heian or Sengoku Japan also unlocks Meiji, but that abandons the Han/Ming engine. If you whiff on Tea, **Qing is the fallback** and the build still functions — you just lose the overbuild payoff below.)
**Ability — Goisshin:** **whenever you overbuild a building, receive Science equal to 25% of the new building's Production cost** (+30% Production toward Dogo Onsen).

This is the punchline. Remember the specialist rework's nasty edge: when the Age turns, specialists sitting on outdated buildings go nearly dead until you overbuild — and Modern-age specialist maintenance is steep. Meiji converts that mandatory chore into a science geyser. You spend the first third of the Modern Age overbuilding your specialist quarters anyway; Meiji pays a quarter of every hammer back in beakers, reactivating specialists *and* funding the sprint to the Launch Pad in one motion. Zaibatsu quarter (Ginkō + Jukogyo) adds gold/production to do it fast. Mikasa and Zero are your "please do not touch" defensive insurance.

## 3. Mementos

Two slots. The ideal loadout is homegrown — Firaxis called these out in the 1.4.0 notes as the sanctioned way to get flat yields back on specialists.

**Ideal:**

- **The Analects** (Confucius level 9): +1 Science per Specialist. Stacks with Keju for +3 flat per head.
- **Brush & Scroll** (Confucius level 2): +5% Growth Rate in Cities per Specialist in that City, max +25%. Specialists feed the growth that makes more specialists. The ouroboros of academia.

**Fallbacks:**

- **Altar Set** (Confucius level 5): +1 Culture per Specialist — eases the pressure, since this build starves Culture a bit.
- **Yasakani no Magatama** (Himiko level 9): +2 Science per Age per Tech Mastery completed — great here since Masteries also feed Innovation, but it's a grind on a different leader. Skip unless you already have it.
- Below Confucius level 9: run **Brush & Scroll + Altar Set**, swap Altar Set → Analects when it unlocks. Both cheap-to-reach, both on the leader you're playing anyway. No grinding required for a functional loadout — that's the point of picking Confucius's own shelf.

## 4. The Opener (turn-by-turn-ish, Antiquity)

**Settle:** fresh water, grassland bias, and pick a home with *defensible geometry* — river bends, mountains, rough terrain between you and the nearest AI. On Deity you don't get to fix a bad neighborhood later; you're choosing your siege map on turn 1.

**Build order:** Scout → Slinger → Settler → Slinger/Warrior → first Science Building → Settler. Two early ranged units, yes. Sovereign let you skip the militia; Deity does not — Independent Powers raid early and AI units carry +8 CS. Upgrade Slingers to **Chu-Ko-Nu** the moment they unlock and consider the army functionally complete.

**Tech order:** Agriculture/Animal Husbandry (gate Masonry) → Pottery → **Writing** (first Science Building — triggers Nine Provinces free pop) → **Currency** (unlocks your first Specialist slot in every City — the build's real "start button") → **Masonry** (Ancient Walls), timed to when your scout says a neighbor is massing. From there: chase every tech with a **Mastery** attached that grants Codices/Innovation. Masteries are victory points now, not flavor.

**Civics:** basics, then live in **Han's unique tree** (Great Wall access + specialist traditions). Government: **Classical Republic** (post-1.4.1 passive Culture in Happy settlements, and its Celebration option of instant +100 Influence is exactly the currency your peace plan runs on). Take **Syncretism** when it appears.

**Settlements:** 3–4 total in Antiquity, placed for food. Structure: **Capital as the One True University**, one secondary city later, everything else stays Towns on Farming focus shipping food home. Towns feeding cities is how you pay the specialist food bill. Don't chase the settlement cap on Deity — every settlement past your defensible core is a hostage.

**Specialists:** the moment Currency lands, pre-place your first specialist on the district where your Science Building sits (ideally the Palace-adjacent quarter). Stack specialists **in the same district** — after an Age transition, one overbuilt building reactivates the whole stack. Plan one glorious science quarter per city and cram it.

**Age goals before transition:** bank Codices (slot them → Innovation), finish science Masteries, complete Scientific Triumphs (Major Triumphs grant Dedications for next Age — take science ones), keep the army small-but-ranged, and enter Exploration unburnt.

## 5. Specialist & City Development

The 1.4.0/1.4.1 rules you're playing under:

- Specialists give **100% of their tile's adjacency yields** + your flat bonuses (Keju +2, Analects +1, traditions, Elective Republic +1). Placement is everything: a specialist on a 6-adjacency quarter is triple one on a 2-adjacency tile.
- **Maintenance scales by Age: 2/4/6 Food AND Happiness each** on current-age and unique Ageless buildings (1/1 if parked on warehouse/obsolete buildings — which also yield almost nothing there).
- Cap: first slot at Currency, raised mainly by **Exploration-era Tech Masteries**, wonders, attribute nodes. Specialists **cannot be unassigned** — every placement is tenure. Literally. Don't hand it to a bad tile.

**The food math:** a Modern city running 8 specialists pays 48 Food and ~48+ Happiness in maintenance. Food comes from your Farming Towns (the Slumlord instinct still applies — towns are the dining hall). Happiness comes from fresh-water settles, improved happiness resources, on-schedule Happiness buildings, Han Great Wall (Ageless Happiness), and — new in 1.4.1 — **stationed Army Commanders boost city Happiness.** Your defensive garrison IS a happiness building.

**Deity wrinkle:** the AI gets +80% Happiness and you don't, and 1.4.1 made Celebrations shorter (10 → 6 turns) and more expensive. Do not build around permanent Celebration uptime — that dream died June 23. Keep specialist cities at **Joyous (20–39 local Happiness)**, treat Ecstatic as gravy, add specialists only while comfortably positive. The moment a city goes Unhappy, you've over-hired.

**Government line:** Classical Republic → **Plutocracy** in Exploration (+20% Production toward Overbuilding passive; its Celebration option gives **Specialists −2 Food/Happiness maintenance during the Celebration** — time hiring sprees to Celebrations) → **Elective Republic** in Modern (passive: **Specialists +1 Science and +1 Culture, +1 Happiness maintenance** — for you that trade is free money; its Celebration can drop +1000 instant Science).

## 6. Defense Without Aggression (the Deity survival kit)

You will likely be declared on at least once. Plan for it like weather.

- **Geometry first.** Your Antiquity settle pattern is your defense budget. Rivers, mountains, marsh — the AI's +8 CS matters much less when it can only bring two units to the front per turn. Chu-Ko-Nu ZOC turns chokepoints into meat grinders.
- **Walls on time, not in panic.** Masonry → Ancient Walls (City Center first, +100 HP, then adjacent districts). Every walled district is a Fortified District that must be individually captured; garrisoned units in them are untouchable except by siege units; defenders get +15 CS retaliation vs melee. Walls lose HP across Age transitions — rebuild Medieval Walls early in Exploration. Since 1.4.0 you can even wall Wonder tiles.
- **The Great Wall(s).** Han's in Antiquity, Ming's in Exploration, chained along the exposed border, both count as Fortifications (+6 CS, 25% cost scaling per segment). Your borders should look like a syllabus: clearly delimited and non-negotiable.
- **War Support is your actual army.** When an AI declares a Surprise War on someone it had decent relations with, it *starts* down on War Support. Every point it's behind = **−1 CS on all its units and Happiness penalties in its cities.** Spend Influence aggressively to swing War Support every round of the auction — this is what your Influence income is *for*. A Deity stack at −3 CS with unhappiness at home frequently just… goes home.
- **Diplomacy as fortification.** Keep active Endeavors with every neighbor (relationship level is what makes attacking you a costly Surprise War). Counter every Denouncement. Befriend Independent Powers early and take suzerainty — buffer states, extra yields, and in Modern a Military city-state suzerainty hands you Partisans. Ally the scariest AI on your continent; on Deity the best wall is somebody else's war.
- **Standing force:** small, ranged, promoted, parked in Fortified Districts and on Wall segments, plus 1–2 Army Commanders (happiness at peace, force multiplier at war). Refresh the roster each Age — obsolete units on Deity are theater props.
- **Never counter-conquer.** Take the peace deal, even a white peace. Occupied cities are unhappiness bombs, warmonger reputation invites pile-ons, and every hammer in a captured city is a hammer not overbuilding a quarter. Defense ends at your border. That's the whole doctrine.

## 7. The Innovation Race (how you actually win under 1.4.0)

Old legacy paths are gone, replaced by Triumphs. The Scientific Victory is now the only *absolute* victory: while Military/Cultural/Economic require holding a score multiple over second place, **Science is a straight race to 100 Innovation, then keep an active Launch Pad alive for a 5-turn countdown.** No opponent-relative threshold. Pure sprint.

**Innovation sources (accrue from turn ~20 onward, not just Modern):**

- **Codices** — earn and slot them (Han's Shi Dafu prints these; your Antiquity head start)
- **Tech Masteries** — take every Mastery with Innovation attached; on the fence, take it anyway
- **Specific techs, civics, and certain Wonders**
- **Special Projects in every Age** (the "chart the stars" / "discover calculus" chain), plus the original three Modern space-race projects
- **A final repeatable project** you can run as many times as needed to close the gap — where your specialist-bloated capital's production+science tsunami cashes out
- Major Scientific **Triumphs** → science **Dedications** at Age transitions. Take them.

**The Modern Age script:** enter the Age → Meiji overbuild blitz across every specialist quarter (reactivating specialists + 25% of every hammer back as Science) → Elective Republic → beeline the deep-tree techs gating the space projects (Flight → Aerodynamics → Rocketry region) → run the project chain, stand up the **Launch Pad**, grind the repeatable in your capital while the countdown ticks. Protect the launch city like your dissertation defense — it should be your best-walled, best-garrisoned city anyway, because it's your capital.

**Deity pacing check:** the AI researches 40% cheaper, so it *will* lead raw tech into mid-Exploration. Its weakness is under-prioritizing Innovation-specific actions. Checkpoints: by end of Antiquity, several Codices slotted and Masteries banked; by end of Exploration, capital pushing triple-digit Science with Ming's +50% and Tea improved ×3; in Modern, first-to-Launch-Pad or within 5 turns of it. Watch the Victory screen's Innovation tally every few turns — if an AI is closing, remember they also have to hold a Launch Pad for 5 turns. A well-timed alliance dragging them into someone else's war is a legal, peaceful, extremely funny form of peer review.

## 8. You Have Misplayed This If…

- **…it's Modern and your specialists are "dormant-adjacent."** Specialists on outdated buildings yield scraps while still eating. If your first ~10 Modern turns weren't an overbuild blitz, you skipped the Meiji ability entirely.
- **…you reach the Modern transition without 3 improved Tea.** No Meiji. You're on the Qing consolation tour. Set a literal reminder when Exploration starts.
- **…a specialist city dips to Unhappy.** You hired past the budget. Post-1.4.1 there's no permanent-Celebration bailout. Stop placing, station a Commander, build the Happiness building you deferred.
- **…Ming is running four Social Policies.** That's −60 Science/turn in the Modern tier of the math (−15 each in Exploration). Traditions or nothing.
- **…an AI Surprise War lands and your Influence bank is empty.** War Support was sitting right there. If you lose the support auction every war, you're funding the wrong yields.
- **…your walls date from an Age ago.** They lost half their HP at transition and the AI's siege units know it.
- **…you own a city you didn't found.** You won a battle and lost the build. This engine has no slot for occupation unhappiness or warmonger reputation.
- **…you're at 100 Innovation with no Launch Pad,** watching a countdown you can't start. The Pad is the bottleneck; the repeatable project is the buffer, not the plan.
- **…it's turn 40 on Deity and you have one Warrior.** The Tenure Track is peaceful, not pacifist. Campus security is part of the endowment.
