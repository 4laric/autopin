# THE SLUMLORD — Civ 7 Build Sheet

*Verified against 1.4.0 "Test of Time" (May 19, 2026). One city. Infinite tenants. Nobody gets a lease upgrade.*

One real city, forever. Every other settlement stays a town, converts its production into gold, and pays rent to the penthouse.

---

## Leader: Augustus

**Imperium Maius:**

- **+2 Production in the Capital for every Town.** Every slum you open makes the penthouse taller. Ten towns = +20 hammers in your only city, free, forever.
- **Purchase Culture Buildings in Towns, with a Gold discount on town purchases** (doubled if the building already exists in the Capital). *Sources conflict on the number — launch-era guides say 50%, newer ones say 15% doubled to 30%. Eyeball the tooltip in-game.*

He's not the biggest raw gold number. He's the best *conversion rate*: towns → capital power, and gold → town infrastructure at a discount. He also unlocks the exact mementos this build wants.

**Runner-up: Xerxes, King of Kings.** +10% Gold in all settlements (doubled in ones you didn't found — captured slums pay double rent), **+1 Settlement Limit per Age**, +3 CS on attack. The aggressive version. His path unlocks **Lotus Blossom** (+4% of Gold income as Science *and* Culture), which patches the build's one real hole.

**Training wheels:** Amina — resource capacity + gold per resource, and her canonical Aksum → Songhai → Mughal path is the guided-tour version of this strategy.

## Civ Path

### Antiquity: Carthage
Not a synergy — a mandate. **Phoenician Heritage: you may only have ONE City and cannot convert Towns to Cities.** The game enforces the bit for you.

- **Colonist** (Settler replacement): every one you make comes with a **second one free**. Buy-one-get-one tenements. Bonus pop if settled adjacent to a resource.
- **Punic Port** (unique quarter): +2 Resource Capacity, **purchasable in Towns**.
- **Cothon / Dockyard:** coastal production and gold for the capital.
- **Numidian Cavalry:** Gold-purchase only, scales with capital resources. Your army *is* your bank account.
- **Sicilian Wars** civic: +2 Settlement Limit, and unlocks the **Suffetes** tradition — **+20% Gold from Mining Towns, +20% Food from Farming/Fishing Towns.** This is your business license. It stays slotted in every age.

### Exploration: Songhai
Massive gold identity, and post-**Kanta** civic they generate **Treasure Convoys from homeland navigable rivers** — colonial-grade money without leaving the neighborhood. Unlock: 3 settlements with 3+ navigable river tiles each (towns count — you'll have ten).

- **Fallback A — Chola:** gold + influence per trade route, huge trade range, treasure fleets. Unlock is usually listed as "3 settlements with coastal City Centers" — *some sources say Cities specifically, which you can't do. Check your unlock screen before committing the game to it.*
- **Fallback B — stay Carthage.** 1.4.0's **Time-Tested Civs** lets you keep Carthage all three ages, with **Syncretism** to steal an Apex civ's infrastructure. The flavor-purist line: the one-city rule stays enforced to the end screen. *Carthage's exact post-Antiquity numbers are unconfirmed — fun option, not verified-optimal.*

### Modern: Mughal India
**Paradise of Nations: +100% Gold from ALL sources, −25% to all other yields except Food.** Read that as the Slumlord: your towns produce only gold and food. **The penalty barely touches you.** Doubled rent, empire-wide. After **Gardens of Paradise**, you can **buy Wonders outright** (+150% cost — you will not notice).

Unlock: Chola leads there directly; otherwise improve 3 Salt tiles, or hold 4 trade routes with unique civs (a trade build does this by accident). Check mid-Exploration.

## The Opening (Antiquity)

**Settle:** coastal, fresh water, resources in ring 1–2. Carthage has a coastal bias — **no coast, no Cothon, no build. Reroll a landlocked start.**

**Techs:** Animal Husbandry or Pottery first (match your tiles), the other second → Sailing / Masonry.

**Civics:** Mysticism (Pantheon) → **Discipline** (first Army Commander — the Zeal seed, do not skip) → **Code of Laws** (Merchants; you get a free extra) → Carthage tree: Shipsheds (Cothon) → Wisdom of Tanit → **Sicilian Wars**.

**Capital build order:** Scout → Scout → **Colonist (~T15–18, arrives as twins)** → Cothon → Colonist → Colonist → wonders/production forever after. From here the capital *builds*; gold buys everything else.

**Towns:** first two down ~T20–25. Coastal, resource-adjacent (+1 pop from Colonist), near neighbors for trade range. Target **6–8 towns by end of Antiquity**, riding the cap: base + techs/civics + Sicilian Wars (+2) + Corona Civica (+1/age).

**Purchase order in each new town:** growth building (Granary/Fishing Quay) → warehouse/production → **Punic Port** → Monument. A Monument in every slum is your entire culture game.

**Pantheon:** God of the Sea, or City Patron Goddess. **Government:** Oligarchy.

## Town Specialization

Towns start as **Growing Town** (+50% growth, keeps its own food). Specializations unlock at **pop 7** *(pre-1.4 number — confirm in-game; the principle holds)*, one choice per Age. Specialized towns ship **all food to connected cities** — and you have exactly one mouth. Every farming town on the continent is DoorDashing calories to your capital.

**Let towns hit ~7–8 pop and claim their key resource tiles FIRST.** Specialize early and you freeze a micro-slum.

- **Antiquity:** ~2 Farming/Fishing : 1 Mining, plus one **Trade Outpost** if trade range is choking. Capital growth is the priority — every citizen is a specialist slot later. Slot **Suffetes**, never unslot it.
- **Exploration:** shift new towns toward **Mining** (~1:1), plus one **Hub Town** (+2 Influence per connected settlement — with your road network that's a firehose, and Influence buys the peace a one-army empire needs).
- **Modern:** everything new or regrown goes **Mining** (Mughal doubles the gold). Keep 3–4 Farming towns feeding the mega-capital.

**Roads are the plumbing.** Food only flows to *connected* cities. Use Merchants' Build Road on anything that didn't auto-connect. An unconnected farming town is a warehouse fire.

**Convert to City: never.**

## The Zeal Parking Lot

**Zeal** (first node, Leadership tree): **+5% to ALL of a settlement's yields while the commander sits on a district or worked tile there — the only commander effect in the game that stacks.**

1. **Discipline civic early** → Commander #1 by ~T20. Commanders only earn XP from fights, so let him escort the early Colonists and bonk hostile independent powers. First promotion → **Zeal** → park him on a capital district. He never moves again. He has a reserved spot.
2. Every subsequent commander: one promotion, Zeal, park. Build them in slow decades, buy them in rich ones.
3. **Stack the capital first** — biggest total yields, and with Augustus + Clipeus it's already a monster. Four Zeal commanders = +20% on everything it does. Once you've got 4–5 there, drop one into each of your top two Mining Towns.
4. They're still commanders. If war comes, the parking lot mobilizes, reinforces with purchased Numidian Cavalry, wins, and returns to the garage with more XP → deeper Leadership tree → better parking.
5. On a Xerxes run: **Scythian Battle-Axe** (L2 memento) pays +1 Gold per Age per Commander XP earned. The parking lot literally generates rent.

## Winning (1.4.0 changed this in your favor)

Economic Victory is now a **GDP threshold race** — accumulate more GDP than everyone and hold a lead over 2nd place (6x early, decaying to 1.25x by late Modern, then a 5-turn countdown). GDP comes from **slotted Resources, Gold Buildings, Treasure Convoys, and Factory Resources.**

This build is a GDP printer: Punic Ports everywhere, capital stuffed with resources (Camels, Colossus, Wisdom of Tanit mastery), Songhai river treasure, Mughal doubling.

*Flag: wiki wording says resources slotted **in Cities** count. Keep your juiciest resources in the capital and watch the GDP tooltip to see whether town slots pay out. If they don't, capital resource capacity is everything — which Carthage's kit maxes anyway.*

## You Have Misplayed This If...

- **...a town is 4 pop in the Exploration Age.** You specialized too early or starved it. Slums must be *crowded*. That's the entire pun.
- **...your capital's food didn't spike when you flipped a farming town.** It's not road-connected. Merchant, road, now.
- **...you're more than 2 over the settlement cap going into a Crisis.** −5 happiness per excess settlement, in EVERY settlement. That's not an empire, that's a class-action lawsuit.
- **...your treasury is under ~500 gold with a hostile neighbor on the border.** You have no standing army; the bank balance IS the army. If you can't emergency-purchase 4 units this turn, you're not rich, you're exposed.
- **...you pressed Convert to City.** Delete the save. You're a developer now, and this town has an HOA.
- **...you're a full era behind in science.** Half a tech tree behind is the cost of doing business (fix with Urban Center towns, capital specialists, or Lotus Blossom on Xerxes). A full era means you forgot the capital is supposed to be a 30+ pop specialist monster.
- **...a commander with Zeal is wandering the map "exploring."** He's not a scout. Back in the parking lot.
