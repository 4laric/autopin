# Civ VII — Confucius / Han: Antiquity Age Plan

## The core synergy (why this pairing snowballs)

Everything about Confucius + Han pushes **population → specialists → science**.

- **Confucius – Keju:** +25% Growth Rate in *all* cities, and +2 Science from every Specialist.
- **Confucius – Guanxi agenda:** he likes leaders with the most Specialists — so playing to your strength also earns diplomacy.
- **Han – Nine Provinces:** your Capital gains Population when it finishes Science buildings; Capital and new Towns get a bonus Population on their first growth. Growth is your engine, and Han feeds it.
- **Han – Shì Dàfū:** a unique civilian built in the Capital at population thresholds; each gives a one-time reward, several of which are **Codices** — the exact currency of the Antiquity Science legacy.
- **Han – Great Wall / Weiyang Palace / Tianxia tradition:** defense, influence, and (Tianxia) even more Science from Specialists later.

**Primary Antiquity target: the Great Library (Science legacy path)** — collect 10 Codices for the Golden Age. **Secondary: Wonders of the Ancient World (Culture)** — wonders are ageless, so anything you build carries into Exploration, and Weiyang Palace fits here.

---

## The one thing that will bite you: happiness

Specialists cost Happiness. Confucius wants lots of them; Antiquity happiness is tight. The whole plan is a balancing act — **don't cram specialists into a city faster than you can feed its happiness** (celebrations, Happiness buildings, resources, the Great Wall's adjacency happiness). If a city goes into unhappiness, growth and yields crater and the strategy stalls.

---

## Opening (roughly turns 1–20)

1. **Settle the Capital on/near fresh water with grassland nearby.** Grassland is required for Weiyang Palace later, and flat, food-rich land maximizes the +25% growth. Prioritize a spot with good Science-building adjacency (rivers, mountains) and a couple of resources.
2. **First build:** a Scout (or two) to find city-state suzerain targets and good town sites — a Science city-state Suzerain bonus is one of your extra Codex sources.
3. **First production priority after scouting:** get toward a Settler / expand early. More settlements = more growth events = more population = more specialists. Aim to plant 2–3 towns quickly.
4. **Warrior/defense:** keep one defender; you don't need an early army. Han's power spikes defensively later with Chu-Ko-Nu.
5. **Buy a Slot/keep an eye on huts and independent peoples** for early gold and codex-adjacent bonuses.

---

## Tech & Civic priority

**Tech:** rush toward the **Science buildings** (Library-tier) — each one built in the Capital triggers Nine Provinces population growth, and they house Codices. Also grab **Writing/early tech that awards Codices** (most Codices come from mastering Tech). Don't neglect the mastery bonuses.

**Civics:** you want three things —

- **Literacy** — an extra Codex source beyond tech.
- Civics that unlock **Celebrations/Happiness** so you can afford specialists.
- Your **Han unique civic tree** (Guanxi, Jin Qing, **Tianxia**). Tianxia (Tier 1) boosts Science from Specialists — pick it up and slot it; it stacks with Keju. These Traditions can be carried into later Ages, so investing here pays off in Exploration too.

Most Wonders require **civic** prerequisites (≈15) rather than tech (≈5), so a strong culture output doubles as your path to the secondary Wonders legacy.

---

## City & town layout

- **Capital = your science/specialist megacity.** Build every Science building here for the Nine Provinces population procs; layer specialists in as happiness allows. Plan district adjacency deliberately — good adjacency is what turns Confucius's flat +2/specialist into huge per-district yields.
- **Towns → convert to specialist-feeder cities** where terrain supports it; otherwise keep them as growth/food towns that funnel population and resources.
- **Great Wall placement:** build segments as a connected line on your frontier. It's **ageless** (permanent across all Ages), acts as a fortification with a defense bonus, and gives **Culture, Tourism, and adjacency Happiness** — that happiness is directly useful for supporting specialists, so it's not just a military structure.
- **Weiyang Palace (Wonder):** place on Grassland; Han gets bonus Production toward it. Big Influence boost — helps with city-states and diplomacy (and counts toward the Wonders legacy).

---

## Shì Dàfū — how to use them

They're built in the Capital once it hits population thresholds, and each reward is one-time and gets more expensive. Since your whole build is growth-first, you'll unlock these faster than most civs. **Prioritize building them whenever available** and steer toward the Codex/Great Work rewards — they plug straight into the Great Library path and slot into your Science buildings. This is effectively a free mini-Great-People pipeline unique to Han.

---

## Antiquity legacy milestones — what to actually chase

**Great Library (Science) — PRIMARY.** Golden Age = **10 Codices**. Sources to combine:

- Tech masteries (the bulk of them)
- The **Literacy** civic
- **Shì Dàfū** rewards
- **Science city-state Suzerain** bonus
- Narrative/event Codices and the Nalanda Wonder if available

**Wonders of the Ancient World (Culture) — SECONDARY.** Golden Age = **7 Wonders**. Even if you don't hit 7, every wonder is ageless value. Lean on your strong culture output (needed for civics anyway) and Weiyang Palace. Grab wonders opportunistically.

Don't spread across all four paths — the Age meter won't let you finish anything. **Commit to Science, dabble in Culture.** Completing a path grants a Golden Age Legacy that carries the bonus into Exploration; failing every milestone in a path opens a Dark Age option instead, so make sure you clear at least the early milestones on Science.

---

## Defense (only if provoked)

You're not a warmonger, but Han defends brutally well. The **Chu-Ko-Nu** (archer replacement) has Zone of Control, higher combat strength, and **+5 ranged strength against adjacent units** — so melee can't safely close on it. Park Chu-Ko-Nu **on Great Wall tiles** for the fortification defense bonus and they'll shred anything that approaches. A small stack of these behind your Wall deters early aggression cheaply, letting you keep spending on growth and science.

---

## Setting up the handoff to Exploration

Confucius actually peaks in the **Exploration Age**, so Antiquity is about building the foundation:

- Bank your **Traditions** (especially Tianxia) to slot forward.
- Finish the **Science legacy** for the Golden Age carryover.
- Keep ageless assets — **Great Wall** and any **Wonders** — since they persist.
- Enter Exploration with high population and specialist infrastructure already humming; the natural Confucius follow-on civ is **Ming** (then Qing in Modern) to continue the science line toward a **Science Victory**.
